    Centre State Division

    Centre-State Matters: The Division deals with Centre-State relations, including working of the constitutional provisions governing such relations, the appointment of Governors, the creation of new States, nominations to Rajya Sabha/Lok Sabha, Inter-State boundary disputes, over-seeing the crime situation in States, the imposition of President's Rule.

    Judicial Matters

    Judicial Matters: Judicial Matter legislative aspects of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), Code of Criminal Procedure (Cr.P.C.) and also the Commission of Inquiry Act. It also handles matters relating to State legislation, which require the assent of the President under the Constitution, political pension to erstwhile rulers before independence, Mercy Petitions under Article 72 of the Constitution

    Judicial and Political Pensions and other residual matters of erstwhile Pol.III Section:-

    • Bills and Cabinet Notes sponsored by other Ministries/Departments for comments of the Ministry of Home Affairs.
    • State Legislative proposals of the following nature:-
      • Bills for consideration of the President under article 201 of the Constitution;
      • Ordinances for instructions of the President under article 213(1) of the Constitution;
      • Bills for the previous sanction of the President under article 304 (b) of the Constitution; and
      • Regulations for Scheduled Areas for the assent of the President under sub-paragraph 5(4) of the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution.
    • Legislative aspects of Commission of Inquiry Act- Amendment of the Act and Rules thereunder;
    • Coordination of Court cases (including summons, judgement etc.) pertaining to matters not specifically assigned to other sections in the Ministry of Home Affairs – forwarding the same to the concerned Ministries/Departments/State Governments. Also coordination of Legal Notices under section 80CPC and summons from courts pertaining to matters not specially assigned to other sections in the MHA including monitoring replies to legal notices u/s 80 CPC etc.
    • The Arrangement with State Governments for defending suits in matters not specifically assigned to other sections.
    • Matter relating to the former rulers of the Indian States and their families arising out of the abolition of privy purse and privileges.
    • Political Pensions.
    • The State Legislations are required to be examined from three angles viz.
      • (i) repugnancy with Central Laws
      • (ii) deviation from National or Central Policy and
      • (iii) legal and Constitutional validity.
        Policy issues involved in the State Legislations are to be sorted out in consultation with the concerned Ministries/Departments as well as State Government. Hence, no time-frame can be fixed in this regard.

    JUDICIAL CELL

    • All matters connected with legislative aspects of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, as amended from time to time, other than the implementation of its provisions.
    • All matters connected with the legislative aspects of the Indian Penal Code, 1860, as amended from time to time other than those
      • which are primarily the concern of other Divisions in the Ministry; and
      • which relate to the implementation of its provisions, i.e. crimes committee under various provisions.
    • Mercy petitions submitted under Article 72 of the Constitution of India for grant pardons, reprieves, respites or remissions of punishment or to suspend, remit or commute the sentence of any person convicted of any offence.
    • Requests for sanction for prosecution under Section 188 Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. (and not under Section 197 Cr.P.C.) other than those from the Central Bureau of Investigation and Foreign Governments.
    • Requests for permission for withdrawal of cases under Section 321 Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 other than those investigated by the Central Bureau of Investigation or launched under Defence of India Rules or Defence of India Security Rules.
    • Write Petitions challenging the validity of the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.
    • Write Petitions challenging the validity of the provisions of the Indian Penal Code other than those which are primarily the concern of other Divisions/ Departments.
    • Examination of Penal provisions in the Central and State Legislations
